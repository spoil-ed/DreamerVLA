\begin{abstract}
Embodied intelligence research mainly follows two directions: Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models and world model–based reinforcement learning methods. VLA models benefit from strong semantic understanding provided by large vision-language models, but they often lack the ability to model environment dynamics and perform long-horizon reasoning. In contrast, world model approaches such as Dreamer can perform long-horizon planning through latent dynamics models, but they typically have weaker perception and limited semantic understanding.

In this work, we explore the DreamerVLA that combines the strengths of both approaches. We first use a VLA encoder to embed multimodal observations, including images, language instructions, and proprioceptive states, into a fixed-length representation. A bottleneck module extracts a compact latent state that contains key physical information. A world model is then trained in the latent space, and an actor–critic module performs imagined rollouts to optimize the policy. This framework aims to integrate semantic perception with model-based planning for embodied decision making.
\end{abstract}
