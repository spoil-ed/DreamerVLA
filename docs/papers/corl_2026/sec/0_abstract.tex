\begin{abstract}
Embodied intelligence research mainly follows two directions: Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models and world model-based reinforcement learning methods. VLA models benefit from strong semantic understanding provided by large vision-language models, but they often lack the ability to model environment dynamics and perform long-horizon reasoning. In contrast, world model approaches such as Dreamer can perform long-horizon planning through latent dynamics models, but they typically have weaker perception and limited semantic understanding. In this work, we explore DreamerVLA, a framework that combines the strengths of both approaches by embedding multimodal observations with a frozen VLA encoder, learning compact latent dynamics, and optimizing an actor-critic policy through imagined rollouts. This framework aims to integrate semantic perception with model-based planning for embodied decision making.
\end{abstract}
